Chees'cale


Chees'cale is a game made in less than 96 hours for the GMTK Game Jam 2024. It's also my first game made in team.

Chees'cale is a kind mix between a climbing game and a runner that respects the theme of the jam: Built to Scale.

In Chees'Cale, you have to scale the giant wall of cheese as a mouse to escape the big bad orange cat ! Dodge obstacles and climb as quickly as possible to the top of the wall !

Try here



How does this game fit the theme?



We decided to interpret the theme in a slightly different way, aiming for originality :

"Scale" can refer to scalling a wall or a mountain. This is the definition we were going for. The core gameplay mechanic is building handles to help the mouse scale the cheese, which is almost exactly the theme definition. The game also plays with scales by the use of the giant wall of cheese and the cat.


How was it made?


The game jam started with a video from the GMTK channel announcing the theme: 'Built to Scale'

Right after, we opened a Figma project to gather our ideas and brainstorm the game concept.


Once we had a clear idea of our game, we had to define project tasks and prioritize them. To do this, we used Trello and ranked priorities from 0 to 5. This was crucial in making sure we delivered a polished game on time.


At that point, each of us got to work on our respective tasks. To manage our code, we opened a GitHub repository where everyone worked on their own branch. We had to merge and resolve some conflicts, as Gamemaker doesn’t handle teamwork on GitHub very well, but it was manageable, especially since only two of us were coding.

As I was familiar with Gamemaker, I coded most of the game's mechanics. I also found some time to work on the sound design. The sound effects were mostly recorded sounds that I processed with various effects on FL Studio. However, I didn’t have time to compose original music, so we used royalty-free tracks from the internet, which we credited on our Itch.io page.

The game's concept is to help the mouse climb the cheese wall to escape the cat. To do this, you must place climbing holds to assist it. However, the panicked mouse gives you a random direction to place the hold, forcing you to work within that constraint. You can also control the intensity of the movement. Placing the hold far makes the mouse jump further, while placing it close results in a smaller move. The goal is to reach the top while avoiding the many obstacles in the way.


This was an incredibly rewarding experience because it was my very first game jam, as well as the first game I made as part of a team. I learned a lot during this jam and I’m very excited to participate again next year.
