Road of the Driver Dancer


Road of the Driver Dancer is a game I developed in Two Weeks during the Summer School at Isart Digital.

It's a rhythm game with a synthwave atmosphere, inspired by Crypt of the Necrodancer, where you must dodge oncoming traffic while driving your car against the flow.

Move from lane to lane to the beat of the music to score as many points as possible.

Try here



How was it made?


Road of the Driver Dancer was developed in just two weeks during an internship, where I learned to use Unity from scratch, having no prior experience with the engine at the beginning of the program.

By the third day, I had to come up with a game idea that would be my project for the remainder of the internship. I’ve always been a big fan of rhythm games, and Crypt of the Necrodancer in particular. Drawing inspiration from things I love, I also looked to Hotline Miami for its synthwave aesthetic.


When working with such a tight deadline, the most important thing is to come up with a simple concept to ensure you can finish it in time. Proper time management is key, and in my case, I used Trello to organize and prioritize all the tasks that needed to be done.


The first challenge was to recreate a beat-based system similar to Crypt of the Necrodancer. In the game, both the player’s and the enemies’ movements are locked to the beat of the music, meaning every move must happen in syncs with the rhythm.


The goal was to implement as many elements tied to the beat as possible, making it easier for the player to stay in sync with the rhythm:

  • The music helps with its drums, it has a kick per beat
  • The visual symbol representing the beat bounce on each beat (a heart in Crypt of the Necrodancer, a synthwave sun here)
  • Beat Bars connect to the beat symbol on every pulse like in Crypt of the Necrodancer
  • The enemies' movement and their bonce are timed to the beat
  • Then the motion of the background is based on the rhythm

Simultaneously, I had to focus on the music. Staying true to the game's atmosphere, I composed a Synthwave track. This was a significant challenge because I had to design the levels while composing the music. Car spawns are tied to the lead synth: a low note triggers a car on the left, while a high note triggers one on the right. Since enemies spawn on the beats, I couldn’t place off-beat notes in the lead. Moreover, I had to envision how the level would play out while composing, making sure not to overwhelm the player with too many consecutive notes.


To program the enemy spawns, I used an Unity tool called NorskaLib Spreadsheets, which connects Google Sheets to Unity, allowing data to be imported in table format.


Each row in the spreadsheet represents the beats, while the columns represent the lanes where enemy cars appear. The "1"s indicate when and where an enemy spawns, according to the beat count and the lane.


One of my key goals was to make the game experience as smooth and non-frustrating as possible. To do this, I implemented a system similar to the "coyote time" mechanic (like in Celeste), where a jump is allowed even after the player steps off the ledge. In Road of the Driver Dancer, players are allowed to move even if they are slightly off-beat or come into contact with an enemy.



There are plenty of other aspects of the game that I could dive into, but I’ve focused here on the most interesting ones. I’ve learned a lot during the development process, and I plan to continue working on and improving the game in the future.



What's next ?
